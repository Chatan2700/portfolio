## The temptation to overbuild

It is easy to turn a portfolio into a full product before the portfolio itself is useful.

I almost started with a dashboard, authentication, a database, and a CMS-like authoring workflow.

That would have been interesting, but it would also delay the real goal: shipping something recruiters can actually see.

## The tradeoff

The first version is intentionally smaller.

It uses files instead of a database, static content instead of a dashboard, and MDX instead of a custom editor.

That tradeoff makes the project easier to finish.

## What comes later

Once the portfolio is live, I can build more advanced projects separately.

The goal is not to avoid complexity forever. The goal is to add complexity when it earns its place.
